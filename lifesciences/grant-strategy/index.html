Research capacity in low- and middle-income countries is often strongest at the science and weakest at the funding layer. Talented researchers at under-resourced universities have strong ideas but limited time, no dedicated grants office, and few international connections to strengthen a proposal's credibility. Kinila partners directly with LMIC faculty and institutions to close that gap — and with HIC researchers who want genuine, not token, LMIC co-investigators on their proposals.

What We Offer

Four ways to work together

Scaled to how much support a proposal actually needs — from a single focused review to an ongoing institutional role.

01

Proposal Strategy Sprint

Focused review of a concept note or draft — reviewer-eye feedback, work package restructuring, and framing for competitiveness.

Best for: a specific upcoming deadline

02

Embedded Co-Development

A named, budgeted role on the proposal team — strategy, structure, and writing support from concept through submission, with continued involvement in implementation if funded.

Best for: high-value, complex, multi-partner bids

03

Institutional Retainer

An ongoing "virtual grants office" function — tracking relevant calls, pre-screening proposals before submission, coaching junior faculty.

Best for: institutions without a research office

04

Cohort Workshops

Structured group sessions on proposal structure, reviewer psychology, and budget logic for multiple faculty at once.

Best for: building capacity across a department

How Engagement Works

Fee structure

Fees are structured as a transparent, budgeted role within the proposal itself, or as an institutional retainer — never as a contingency or success fee, in line with standard funder and institutional conflict-of-interest norms. Where a project succeeds, we remain available to support implementation, not just the paperwork that got it funded.
