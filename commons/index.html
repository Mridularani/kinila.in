About Kinila Commons

Knowledge as a shared resource

Kinila Commons is a community library in Bailore, Udupi — a place where anyone can come to read, explore ideas, and borrow books. We believe that access to knowledge should not depend on where you live or what you can afford.

The library holds over 1,500 books across fiction, non-fiction, history, science, cookery, and more — all catalogued and available. Reading on-site is always free. Members can borrow books and take them home.

Kinila Commons is one of three arms of Kinila Ventures, a partnership firm working to make science and knowledge accessible to people who need it most.

How it works

Simple, open, and community-run

Whether you want to spend an afternoon reading or borrow a book to take home, Kinila Commons is open to you.

01

Browse the catalogue

Search our collection of 1,500++ books below. Filter by genre, category, or search by title or author to find something that interests you.

02

Visit and read — free

Come to the library and read any book on-site, at any time during opening hours. No registration, no fee. Just walk in.

03

Register to borrow

To take books home, register as a member. A small annual membership fee applies. Members may borrow books for up to 2 weeks.

04

Return and repeat

Return borrowed books on time so others can enjoy them too. Members in good standing can borrow again immediately.

Donate books

Share the books you love

Kinila Commons grows through the generosity of the community. If you have books at home that you have read and would like others to enjoy, we welcome donations.

We accept books in good or fair condition across all genres — fiction, non-fiction, children's books, cookery, history, and more. Books in Kannada, Hindi, and other Indian languages are especially welcome.

To donate, bring your books to the library or contact us to arrange a drop-off.

The bigger vision

More than a library — a place to belong
